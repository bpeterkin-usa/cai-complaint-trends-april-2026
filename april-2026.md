## 12. Targeted Themes for Business Engagement

Based on April data, the following five themes are prioritized:

### Theme 1: Trading Execution (time/price) — 4th Consecutive Month of Acceleration (26 complaints, +100% vs Q1 avg)

What Customers Are Telling Us: Customers are alleging that executed prices differ significantly from the price displayed at order submission. The pattern is consistent across multiple customers: they see Price A, submit an order, and receive Price B — often $1-15 different. Multiple customers provide specific dollar amounts and stock names. This is no longer a low-volume signal; it has doubled its Q1 average and shows no sign of plateauing.

Customer Impact: Customers report direct financial loss from price discrepancies. Some reference multiple stocks affected in a single day. Others describe watching the price in real-time and seeing the executed price differ from what was displayed moments before.

Guided Questions:
- Root Cause: What changed between January (10) and April (26)? Is this correlated with market volatility, new order routing, or quote refresh timing?
- Customer Expectation: Are customers placing market orders expecting limit-order behavior? If so, is the UI adequately distinguishing order types?
- Ability to Act: Can quote refresh frequency be increased? Can order confirmation show expected vs actual price before final execution?
- Planned Actions: What actions (if any) are planned?
- Outcome Measurement: Target reversal to 15/month or below (Q1 average). When should that be observed?


### Theme 2: Trading Cancel — New Spike (17 complaints, +467% vs Q1 avg)

What Customers Are Telling Us: Customers are alleging they attempted to cancel orders but the cancellation either did not process, processed too late (order already executed), or the app did not provide a functional cancellation mechanism. Multiple customers describe pressing cancel and nothing happening, or being told the order is "expired" but funds remain locked.

Customer Impact: Customers report being stuck in positions they did not intend to hold, with financial exposure from orders they tried to stop. Some describe urgent financial need for the locked funds.

Guided Questions:
- Root Cause: Is this a UI issue (cancel button not visible or functional), a timing issue (orders execute before cancel processes), or a system issue (cancellations failing silently)?
- Customer Expectation: Do customers understand the window for cancellation? Is it communicated clearly?
- Ability to Act: Can the cancel flow be audited for reliability? Can confirmation of successful cancellation be surfaced more clearly?
- Planned Actions: What actions (if any) are planned?
- Outcome Measurement: Target return to 5/month or below. When should that be observed?


### Theme 3: Account Lock — 5th Consecutive Monthly Increase (35 complaints, 25 to 27 to 30 to 35)

What Customers Are Telling Us: Customers are alleging their accounts are locked without explanation, preventing access to positions. The unbroken 5-month upward trend suggests a systemic driver rather than one-time events.

Customer Impact: Customers report inability to sell during market movements, inability to access funds, and no clarity on what triggered the lock or how to resolve it.

Guided Questions:
- Root Cause: What is driving the sustained increase? Has a policy threshold changed? Is fraud detection sensitivity increasing?
- Customer Expectation: Are customers receiving proactive communication when locked? Do they know what action to take?
- Ability to Act: Can lock communications include reason and resolution steps? Can false-positive rate be reduced?
- Planned Actions: What actions (if any) are planned?
- Outcome Measurement: Target reversal of the upward trend (30/month or below). When should that be observed?


### Theme 4: Activity - Sales Proceeds — 4th Consecutive Month of Acceleration (11 complaints, 3 to 5 to 8 to 11)

What Customers Are Telling Us: Customers are alleging that after selling stocks, the proceeds do not appear in their cash balance for 2-3+ days — or in some cases, not at all. Multiple customers describe selling and then having no money reflected anywhere in the app. Some report being charged fees on sales they haven't received proceeds for.

Customer Impact: Customers report being unable to reinvest or withdraw funds they believe they've already received from a completed sale. Some describe urgent financial need and frustration at being told "one day" when it takes three or more.

Guided Questions:
- Root Cause: Is this a settlement timing issue (T+1 not communicated), a display issue (funds exist but aren't shown), or a processing failure?
- Customer Expectation: Do customers understand settlement periods? Is the app communicating "pending" status clearly?
- Ability to Act: Can a "funds pending — available [date]" indicator be added post-sale?
- Planned Actions: What actions (if any) are planned?
- Outcome Measurement: Target 5/month or below. When should that be observed?


### Theme 5: Online - Navigation — Surging (22 complaints, 9 to 4 to 13 to 22)

What Customers Are Telling Us: Customers are alleging they cannot find basic features in the app — portfolio view, buy/sell buttons, cancel functionality, account settings. The April surge (+69% from March) suggests a possible app update or UI change that disrupted familiar navigation patterns.

Customer Impact: Customers report functional inability to execute trades or view positions because they cannot locate the interface elements needed to do so. Some describe trying to cancel orders but being unable to find the option.

Guided Questions:
- Root Cause: Was there a recent app update or UI redesign? Did navigation paths change?
- Customer Expectation: Are customers looking for features that moved or were renamed?
- Ability to Act: Can in-app guidance or tooltips be added post-update? Can the previous navigation path be restored or redirected?
- Planned Actions: What actions (if any) are planned?
- Outcome Measurement: Target return to 10/month or below (Q1 baseline). When should that be observed?
